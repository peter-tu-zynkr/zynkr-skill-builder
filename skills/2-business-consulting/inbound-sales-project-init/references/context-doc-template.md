# Context Doc Template

This is the canonical structure for the Google Doc that lives inside every inbound sales project folder. Fill the placeholders (anything in `<…>`) with values from the email. Keep the section order and headings — consistency across projects is the whole point.

The doc is meant to be **the single place** the user looks at to remember what the inbound is and what we owe back. It should read top-to-bottom in 60 seconds.

---

## Template

```
<Project Name> · 脈絡彙整
=====================================

最後更新：<YYYY-MM-DD>
狀態：Consult intake
Notion ticket：<paste after Step 5, or leave as "建立中" on first pass>

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Inbound 來源
------------------------------------------------------------

From：<Sender name + email>
Date：<YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM (Day)>
Subject：<verbatim subject line>
Gmail thread：<https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#all/THREAD_ID>
附件：<filename.pdf (size)> · (or "無" if no attachments)

------------------------------------------------------------
2. <Sender 名字> 原始信件全文
------------------------------------------------------------

<paste the email body verbatim — do not paraphrase, do not summarize,
do not strip pleasantries. This section is the source of truth that
future-you can always fall back to.>

------------------------------------------------------------
3. 提案核心拆解
------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 目標受眾
   · <who this is for>

3.2 切入角度
   · <how they're framing it>

3.3 內容定位
   · <what shape of deliverable they're imagining>

3.4 商業轉換邏輯
   · <how this connects to revenue / funnel — if applicable>

3.5 <Sender> 想要的回饋
   · <the specific ask they made of you>

------------------------------------------------------------
4. 待釐清議題 (Open Questions)
------------------------------------------------------------

· <each open question on its own line — things you can't answer from the email alone>

------------------------------------------------------------
5. 後續動作 (Next Actions)
------------------------------------------------------------

· [ ] <discrete action with an owner implied>
· [ ] Review 附件並彙整重點
· [ ] 起草 reply
· [ ] 決定參與深度

------------------------------------------------------------
6. 工作脈絡 (滾動更新)
------------------------------------------------------------

<empty on creation — this is where future updates, decisions, and
conversation logs accumulate over the life of the project>
```

## Language note

The template is in Chinese because most inbounds Peter receives are in Chinese. For English inbounds, translate the section headings (e.g., `1. Inbound Source`, `2. <Sender>'s Original Letter`, `3. Proposal Breakdown`, etc.) but keep the same structure. Mixed-language inbounds: use the email's primary language for the breakdown sections — readers shouldn't have to mentally translate between the email and the summary.

## What goes where

- **Section 1** is the cold-open metadata — anyone scanning should grok "what is this and when did it come in" in 5 seconds.
- **Section 2** is verbatim on purpose. Resist the urge to clean it up. Pleasantries, typos, and tone all carry signal.
- **Section 3** is your interpretation of what they're actually proposing. Five sub-points keep it scannable.
- **Section 4** is for things you don't yet know but will need to. If section 4 is empty, you probably haven't thought hard enough.
- **Section 5** is the action list. Items here should be moveable into the Notion ticket's checklist.
- **Section 6** stays empty on day one and grows over time. It's the working journal for the project.
